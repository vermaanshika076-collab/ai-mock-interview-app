Resume Tips

Real-world insights to build a resume recruiters actually read

How Recruiters View Your Resume

Recruiters spend **6–10 seconds** scanning a resume. Your resume must be **clear, relevant, and easy to scan** — not decorative or lengthy.

Resume Structure That Works

Keep It One Page (For Freshers)

A one-page resume is preferred for students and fresh graduates. Focus on skills, projects, and internships rather than long descriptions.

Use Clear Section Headings

Common sections recruiters expect:
• Summary
• Skills
• Projects / Experience
• Education

Content That Makes an Impact

Write a Strong Summary

Your summary should explain **who you are, what you know, and what you want** — in 2–3 lines.

Highlight Skills Relevant to the Job

Always tailor your skills section based on the role you’re applying for. Irrelevant skills dilute your resume.

Projects Matter More Than Certificates

Recruiters prefer **real projects** that demonstrate problem-solving over multiple certificates.

Resume Do’s & Don’ts

Do’s

  • Use simple, professional fonts
  • Quantify achievements where possible
  • Customize resume for each job
  • Use bullet points for readability
  • Keep formatting consistent

Don’ts

  • Don’t add unnecessary personal details
  • Don’t use flashy colors or graphics
  • Don’t exaggerate skills
  • Don’t write paragraphs
  • Don’t submit without proofreading

Real Recruiter Insight

“A clean, honest resume with real projects stands out more than a fancy design with no substance.”

Final Resume Checklist

  • ✔ Resume limited to one page
  • ✔ Job-relevant skills highlighted
  • ✔ Projects clearly explained
  • ✔ No spelling or grammar mistakes
  • ✔ Professional email ID
  • ✔ Saved as PDF before sharing
